Troubleshooting

  • Audio pronunciation not playing Ensure device volume is up, Silent Mode is off, and Low Power Mode is disabled. Try switching between different reciters or restart the app.
  • Flashcards not flipping or responding Try closing and reopening the app. Clear the app cache in Settings if the issue persists.
  • Progress not saving Ensure you're signed in and have an internet connection at least once for cloud sync. Local progress saves automatically when online.
  • Matching game freezes or crashes Restart the app. If it continues, try reinstalling the app (your cloud-synced progress will be preserved if you're signed in).
  • Arabic text not displaying correctly Ensure your device supports Arabic script. Update to the latest iOS version for best compatibility.
  • Bookmarks disappeared Sign in to restore bookmarks from cloud sync. Guest mode stores bookmarks locally only.

FAQs

  • How do flashcards work? Tap a flashcard to flip between Arabic and English. click circle button in top right corner to mark as card as completed, then scroll down to see completed cards.
  • Can I use the app offline? Yes. All flashcards, vocabulary, and verses work offline, Audio plays once online, then cached.
  • How do I switch reciters? Go to Settings and select your preferred reciter from the audio options. Multiple reciters are available for pronunciation practice.
  • What's the difference between flashcards and matching games? Flashcards help you learn new vocabulary. Matching games test your knowledge by pairing Arabic words with English translations under time pressure.
  • How is my progress tracked? The app tracks completed flashcards, game scores, and bookmarked verses. Signed-in users sync across devices; guest mode saves locally only.
  • Can I bookmark specific verses? Yes. Tap the bookmark icon on any verse to save it for quick access later. Find all bookmarks in your profile.
  • Which translation is used? The Noble Quran by Dr. Mustafa Khattab (The Clear Quran), chosen for its accuracy and clarity.
  • Is this for complete beginners? Yes. The app is designed for beginners and intermediate learners with no prior Arabic knowledge required.
